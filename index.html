Research Overview

My Ph.D. research develops minimally invasive, AI-powered diagnostic protocols for plant disease surveillance, specializing in multiclass disease detection and pixel-level severity quantification through advanced image segmentation. I integrate deep learning architectures (e.g., YOLO, MobileNet, SAM) with attention-based enhancements for real-time field deployment, achieving simultaneous detection, symptom discrimination, and severity estimation while reducing manual assessment time by 12-fold. By combining thermal imaging for pre-symptomatic detection, RGB-based mobile disease indexing, CRISPR diagnostics, and epidemiological modeling, my work establishes non-destructive monitoring frameworks that bridge laboratory precision with scalable field implementation for sustainable, chemical-free management of plant pathogens.
